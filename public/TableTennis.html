Table tennis, also known as ping pong, is a fast-paced indoor sport played between two or four players. The game is played on a flat rectangular table, usually 2.74 meters long, 1.525 meters wide, and 0.76 meters high, divided into two equal halves by a net. Players use small rackets (also called paddles or bats) to hit a lightweight ball (weighing about 2.7 grams) back and forth across the table.

Here are the basics of the game:
  • Objective: The goal is to score points by making the ball land on the opponent's side of the table in a way that they are unable to return it.
  • Serving: The game begins with a serve. The server must throw the ball upward and hit it so that it bounces once on their side and then on the opponentâ€™s side.
  • Rallying: After the serve, players continue to hit the ball back and forth, allowing it to bounce once on their side before returning it.
  • Scoring: A point is scored when the opponent fails to return the ball, misses the table, hits it into the net, or commits a fault. The game typically goes to 11 or 21 points, depending on the rules used, and a player must win by at least 2 points.
  • Gameplay: Matches are usually best-of-five or best-of-seven games.
Table tennis is an Olympic sport and is known for its quick reflexes, precision, and strategy. Players can use various techniques like spins, smashes, and loops to control the ball and gain an advantage over their opponents.
